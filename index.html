Abstract

The creation of 4D avatars from text typically leverages the text-to-image (T2I) diffusion models as prior to synthesize 3D avatars in canonical space and subsequently applies animation with target motions. However, such optimization-byanimation paradigm has several drawbacks. For pose-agnostic optimization, the rendered images in canonical pose for naive Score Distillation Sampling (SDS) exhibit domain gap and can hardly preserve view-consistency using only T2I priors. For posthoc animation, simply copy the source motions to 3D targets yields translation artifacts and misalignment. To address these issues, we propose Skeleton-aware Text-to-4D Avatar generation with in-network motion Retargeting model (STAR). STAR considers the geometry and skeleton difference between the template and target, which leverages the advances of motion retargeting with modified SDS to progressively optimize the geometry, texture, and motion end-to-end. By leveraging the skeleton-conditioned T2I and T2V priors, we propose hybrid SDS to provide view and frame consistent supervision signals conditioned by the informatively retargeted and occlusion-aware skeleton. With the above insights and techniques, we present quantitative and qualitative experiments to demonstrate our novel STAR can synthesize high-quality 4D avatars with vivid animations that well align with the text description, and present ablation studies to show the contributions of each component in STAR.

Overview of the proposed STAR. Left. Given a text description, we initialize the human motion with pretrained text-to-motion model. Note that the typical optimization-by-animation paradigm easily yields deteriorated body structures and animation artifacts for 4D avatar generation. Right. We eliminate the potential pose distribution bias in the SDS-based optimization by integrating the retargeted motion for animation. With the personalized and occlusion-aware skeleton, we leverage the hybrid T2I and T2V diffusion models to provide 3D consistent priors that progressively optimize the geometry, texture, and motion to produce 4D avatar in an end-to-end manner.
